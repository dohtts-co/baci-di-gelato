## Brand Identity (Logo — Source of Truth)

A delicate single-line illustration of a couple sharing a kiss, each holding an ice cream cone, rendered in minimal continuous-line art (not painterly, not filled).

Wordmark "BACI DI GELATO" set in a clean serif/sans with wide letter-spacing and small bullet-point dividers ("• BACI DI GELATO •"), subtitle "ICE CREAM KISSES" in smaller spaced-out caps underneath.

Logo exists in two value modes: white linework on black, and black linework on white.

## Design Direction

- **Primary palette**: high-contrast black and white, driven by the existing line-art logo. The pastel/watercolor aesthetic (blush pink, cream, sage, butter yellow, watercolor washes) should appear only as a light secondary accent, e.g. a soft blush wash behind a section, never competing with the black/white identity.
- **Typography**: wide-letter-spaced serif or sans for headings, echoing the wordmark's spacing and small-caps treatment. Avoid generic warm-cream-serif-terracotta AI defaults.
- **Illustration style**: any icons or dividers should feel hand-drawn/single-line to match the logo, not flat vector icons.
- **Mood**: intimate, romantic, artisanal, not bright/playful/candy-colored.
- **Structure**: clearly distinguish the two branches (parlour vs event hire) without making either feel secondary, plus a flavour showcase and testimonials section.
- **Signature element**: lean into the "kiss"/couple motif as the one memorable visual idea, e.g. a hand-drawn divider or subtle line-art accent near the hero, rather than introducing an unrelated gimmick.

## Additional Requirements

- **Dynamic**: the page should feel alive, not static, consider scroll-triggered reveals, subtle hover micro-interactions, or an orchestrated page-load moment.
- **Presentable scrolling aesthetic**: designed with a clear scroll narrative in mind, each section should feel like a deliberate "next beat" rather than a stacked list of blocks.
- **Simple**: restrained execution, no visual clutter, generous whitespace, one signature moment rather than many competing effects.
- **Cute**: warmth and charm should come through, in tone of copy, in the logo's romantic illustration, and in small personality details, but without tipping into pastel/playful candy branding that conflicts with the black-and-white identity.
- **Informative**: all key content (locations, contact info, flavours, testimonials, hours/visit info) must be easy to find while scrolling, nothing should be sacrificed for aesthetics alone.

## Refined: Typography (Resolved)

- **Headings**: Cormorant Garamond — italic weight, 300–400, wide-spaced. Echoes the elegance of the wordmark without being a direct copy.
- **Labels / all-caps / navigation**: Cormorant SC (small caps variant) — letter-spacing 0.3–0.5em, 0.65–0.85rem. Used for section labels, nav links, branch type tags.
- **Body**: Cormorant Garamond regular — 1rem–1.15rem, line-height 1.8–1.9.
- **No generic sans-serif body text** — the entire page stays within the serif family for brand coherence.

## Refined: Animation Spec

- **Hero load**: illustration → wordmark → divider → subtitle → tagline → location, each fading up with 0.2s stagger, starting at 0.3s delay. Scroll indicator pulses in last.
- **Scroll reveals**: `IntersectionObserver` at 15% threshold. Elements fade up 30px over 0.8s. Children within a section stagger with 0.1s increments via CSS delay classes.
- **Branch card hover**: background transitions from white to blush over 0.4s.
- **Flavour list hover**: item slides right 0.5rem, dash extends from 12px to 20px.
- **Scroll indicator**: the vertical line pulses scale(1) → scale(1.3) → scale(1) on a 2s loop.
- No parallax, no JS-heavy effects — CSS transitions and IntersectionObserver only.

## Competitor Analysis (Deep Scan, June 2026)

Scanned: artisan gelaterias and UK wedding/event gelato cart hire businesses — the two
segments Baci Di Gelato operates in. Goal: identify what each does well and what transfers
to our build.

### Gelato Village (Leicester) — artisan parlour
- **Unique strength**: credibility through *credentials, not flash*. Displays "Slow Food UK"
  and "Compagnia Gelatieri" memberships, "since 2014", educational "What is gelato?" content.
- **Transferable**: an education beat ("Not just ice cream") and authenticity badges. We already
  have a stronger version — a *national award* (Golden Scoop 2026) and a named mentor (Jonathan Susca).
  Lean on these the way they lean on memberships.

### Snugburys (Cheshire) — family artisan ice cream
- **Unique strength**: *warmth through story + playful naming*. "Churned with love on our family
  farm", "Scoopin' smiles since '86", "40 years in the making", named flavours (Snugtella).
  Origin story moves product-discovery → family journey to build emotional connection.
- **Transferable**: our family story (Erin & Jonathan + son) is our equivalent of the farmhouse
  origin. Give it a dedicated, photo-led beat. Playful flavour names (Unicorn, Eton Mess) are
  already on-brand — present them with personality, not as a flat list.

### Adriano's Gelato (Hampshire) — wedding/event cart hire
- **Unique strength**: *removes friction for event bookers*. All-inclusive transparent pricing,
  explicit practical details (cart dimensions, electrical requirements), clear geographic coverage,
  "65 flavours", custom flavour creation, four named occasions (Weddings/Parties/Anniversaries/Events).
- **Transferable**: event clients need logistics answered. Add a short "how event hire works" /
  practical-reassurance line and make the enquiry CTA frictionless. We already offer bespoke flavour
  creation and year-round UK coverage — state both plainly near the event CTA.

### WA Carr & Son / Berlotti — branded cart presentation
- **Unique strength**: the *cart itself as the hero object* — branded Piaggio/vintage cart photographed
  in situ at events, "cater for 40 to 500+".
- **Transferable**: our vintage cart with the line-art logo on it (see wedding-cart photo) is a genuine
  visual asset. Feature the branded cart prominently in the event-hire beat rather than generic gelato.

### Directory platforms (Poptop, Add to Event)
- Most cart-hire businesses are discovered through directories, not their own site. Implication: our
  site's job is *conversion + credibility* once someone arrives — award, real testimonials, real photos,
  easy enquiry — not SEO discovery. Design accordingly.

### Net takeaways for our build
1. **Credibility cluster near the top** — award + mentor + 5★ reviews, the way Gelato Village front-loads memberships.
2. **Family origin story as an emotional beat** — Snugburys' strongest move; we have the photos for it.
3. **Event hire must answer logistics + frictionless enquiry** — Adriano's strength; reduce booker hesitation.
4. **The branded vintage cart is a hero object** — photograph it in situ, don't hide it.
5. **We out-credential most competitors** (national award, named mentor) — quiet-luxury restraint will make us
   look more premium than the "65 flavours / Wow your guests!" enthusiastic-but-generic tone they use.
